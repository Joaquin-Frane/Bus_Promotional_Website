Basic Information

Manila to Baler is approximately a 5 to 6-hour drive, covering around 250 kilometers via the North Luzon Expressway (NLEX) and the Aurora Highway. Baler, located in Aurora province, is known as the birthplace of surfing in the Philippines, attracting both beginners and experienced surfers to its beautiful waves and beaches. The area is also rich in history, with sites such as the historic Baler Church and the Museo de Baler showcasing the town's cultural heritage. Additionally, Baler offers stunning natural attractions, including waterfalls, lush forests, and scenic coastlines, making it a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts and nature lovers.

Trivia

The drive from Manila to Baler takes you through scenic landscapes, including mountains, rivers, and coastal views, showcasing the beauty of the Aurora province. Baler gained fame as the site where the first surfing competitions were held in the Philippines, with Sabang Beach being a popular surfing spot. The town is also historically significant, known for the Siege of Baler, where Spanish soldiers held out for nearly a year during the Philippine Revolution. Additionally, Baler is surrounded by stunning natural attractions, including the beautiful Ditumabo Falls and the picturesque Costin Beach, making it a haven for adventure seekers and nature lovers.
