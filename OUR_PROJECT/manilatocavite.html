Basic Information

Manila to Cavite is a popular route for commuters and travelers, located approximately 30 to 40 kilometers south of Metro Manila. The journey typically takes about 1 to 2 hours by car or bus, depending on traffic conditions. Cavite is known for its rich history, particularly its role in the Philippine Revolution, and is home to several historical sites and attractions.

Trivia

Cavite is known as the "Historical Capital of the Philippines," as it was the birthplace of the Philippine Revolution against Spanish rule. Additionally, Cavite is famous for its unique delicacies, such as bibingka (rice cake) and puto bumbong (purple rice cake), which are popular during the Christmas season. The area is also known for its scenic spots, particularly Tagaytay, which offers breathtaking views of Taal Volcano and Lake.
