Basic Information

Manila to Vigan is approximately a 7 to 8-hour drive, covering around 400 kilometers via the North Luzon Expressway (NLEX) and the MacArthur Highway. Vigan, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is famous for its well-preserved Spanish colonial architecture and cobblestone streets. The city is known for its rich cultural heritage, featuring landmarks such as Calle Crisologo, Vigan Cathedral, and the Bantay Bell Tower. Vigan is also famous for its local delicacies, including empanada and longganisa, making it a popular destination for food lovers.

Trivia

The journey from Manila to Vigan takes you through the picturesque landscapes of Luzon, showcasing rice fields and mountainous regions. Vigan is renowned for being one of the best-preserved examples of a Spanish colonial town in Asia, earning its UNESCO World Heritage status in 1999. The city is also famous for its unique "kalesa" (horse-drawn carriage) rides, offering a charming way to explore its historic streets. Additionally, Vigan is known for its vibrant pottery industry, where visitors can see artisans at work crafting traditional "burnay" jars.
