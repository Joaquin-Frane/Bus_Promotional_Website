Basic Information

Manila to Tarlac covers a distance of approximately 120 kilometers, typically taking 2 to 3 hours by car or bus via the North Luzon Expressway (NLEX) and Subic-Clark-Tarlac Expressway (SCTEX). Tarlac is known for its rich history, agricultural lands, and cultural significance in Central Luzon. It offers a mix of historical landmarks, outdoor activities, and culinary delights, including its famous "inatô" cuisine. The province serves as a gateway to northern Luzon, making it a common stop for travelers heading further north.

Trivia

The route from Manila to Tarlac was historically significant during the Bataan Death March in World War II, with Capas, Tarlac being one of the final destinations for the Filipino and American soldiers.Tarlac is known as the birthplace of the Philippines’ first female president, Corazon Aquino, whose family has deep roots in the province.The completion of the Subic-Clark-Tarlac Expressway (SCTEX) greatly improved access to the region, boosting tourism and trade. Tarlac is also home to the famous Mount Pinatubo, known for its 1991 eruption and now a popular hiking destination.
