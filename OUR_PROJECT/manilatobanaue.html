Basic Information

Manila to Banaue is approximately a 9 to 10-hour drive, covering around 350 kilometers via the Maharlika Highway. Banaue is renowned for its stunning rice terraces, often referred to as the "Eighth Wonder of the World," which have been cultivated for over 2,000 years by the Ifugao people. The town serves as a cultural hub, showcasing traditional Ifugao architecture and customs, with opportunities for trekking and immersing in local culture. Banaue is also a UNESCO World Heritage Site, attracting visitors interested in its breathtaking landscapes and rich indigenous heritage.

Trivia

The journey from Manila to Banaue takes you through mountainous terrain and scenic landscapes, showcasing the beauty of Northern Luzon. Banaue's rice terraces are not only a UNESCO World Heritage Site but also a testament to the ingenuity and hard work of the Ifugao people, who developed this agricultural system without modern tools. The terraces are still actively farmed today, producing rice that is an essential part of the local culture and cuisine. Additionally, Banaue is known for its vibrant festivals, such as the Pahiyas Festival, where locals showcase their agricultural products and traditional crafts in colorful displays.
