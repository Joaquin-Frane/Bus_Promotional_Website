Basic Information

Manila to La Union is a popular route in the Philippines, covering around 270 kilometers north of the capital. The trip usually takes 4 to 6 hours by car or bus via the North Luzon Expressway (NLEX) and Tarlac-Pangasinan-La Union Expressway (TPLEX). La Union is a renowned beach destination, particularly for surfing enthusiasts, with spots like San Juan Beach. Aside from its beaches, La Union also boasts historical sites and local delicacies, making it a favored getaway.

Trivia

The Manila to La Union route has historical significance as it passes through key battle sites from World War II in northern Luzon. La Union is known as the "Surfing Capital of the North," with its waves attracting both local and international surfers. The completion of the Tarlac-Pangasinan-La Union Expressway (TPLEX) significantly reduced travel time to the province, making it more accessible to tourists. La Union is also home to the century-old Pindangan Ruins, a remnant of a 1764 church destroyed by an earthquake.
