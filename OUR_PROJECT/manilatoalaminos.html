Basic Information

Manila to Alaminos is approximately a 5 to 6-hour drive, covering around 250 kilometers via the North Luzon Expressway (NLEX) and Tarlac-Pangasinan-La Union Expressway (TPLEX). Alaminos is best known as the gateway to the Hundred Islands National Park, a popular destination featuring 124 islands and islets with stunning beaches and diverse marine life. The city is also famous for its delicious seafood and local delicacies, making it a great spot for food lovers. Visitors can enjoy various activities such as island hopping, snorkeling, and hiking, making Alaminos an ideal destination for adventure seekers and nature enthusiasts.

Trivia

The drive from Manila to Alaminos takes you through scenic landscapes, including rice fields and rural towns in Pangasinan. Alaminos is home to the Hundred Islands National Park, established in 1940, which is one of the Philippines' first national parks and offers diverse ecosystems. The city hosts the annual Hundred Islands Summer Festival, celebrating local culture with parades, contests, and beach activities. Additionally, Alaminos is known for its vibrant seafood market, where visitors can enjoy fresh catches, including the famous "bangus" (milkfish).
