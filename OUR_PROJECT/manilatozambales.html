Basic Information

Manila to Zambales is approximately a 3 to 4-hour drive, covering a distance of about 200 kilometers depending on traffic and the exact destination in Zambales. The route typically follows the North Luzon Expressway (NLEX) and Subic-Clark-Tarlac Expressway (SCTEX). Zambales is known for its beautiful beaches, including Anawangin Cove and Potipot Island, and is a popular destination for outdoor activities like camping and hiking. Public transport options include buses from Manila's terminals heading to major towns in Zambales like Olongapo and Iba.

Trivia

The drive from Manila to Zambales offers a scenic route through expressways like NLEX and SCTEX, with views of rolling hills and farmland. Zambales is home to the famous Mt. Pinatubo, whose 1991 eruption was one of the largest in the 20th century. The province is also rich in history, with Subic Bay playing a significant role as a former U.S. naval base. Additionally, Zambales is the top producer of mangoes in the Philippines, known for its sweet "Zambales mangoes."
