Basic Information

Manila to Pagudpud is approximately a 10 to 12-hour drive, covering around 550 kilometers, typically via the North Luzon Expressway (NLEX) and the Maharlika Highway. Pagudpud, located in Ilocos Norte, is known for its stunning white sand beaches, crystal-clear waters, and picturesque landscapes. Popular attractions include the famous Saud Beach, the scenic Bangui Windmills, and the breathtaking Patapat Viaduct. Pagudpud is also rich in natural beauty, featuring waterfalls, caves, and lush green hills, making it a perfect destination for nature lovers and adventure seekers.

Trivia

The journey from Manila to Pagudpud takes you through the beautiful landscapes of Northern Luzon, showcasing rice fields, mountains, and coastal scenery. Pagudpud is often referred to as the "Boracay of the North" due to its pristine beaches, particularly Saud Beach, which is less commercialized than its southern counterpart. The Bangui Windmills, the first power-generating windmill farm in Southeast Asia, are located just a short drive away, making it a popular stop for visitors. Additionally, Pagudpud is home to the picturesque Patapat Viaduct, a coastal bridge offering stunning views of the ocean and mountains.
