Basic Information

Metro Manila to Bataan is approximately 120 kilometers northwest, making it a popular destination for both day trips and longer vacations. The travel time typically ranges from 2 to 3 hours by car or bus, primarily via the North Luzon Expressway (NLEX) and the Subic-Clark-Tarlac Expressway (SCTEX). Bataan is known for its rich history, particularly during World War II, as it was the site of the Bataan Death March and is home to several memorials and historical sites. The province also boasts beautiful natural attractions, including beaches, mountains, and the picturesque Mount Samat, which features a large cross monument and panoramic views of the surrounding areas.

Trivia

The route from Metro Manila to Bataan is historically significant as it was part of the Bataan Death March during World War II, where thousands of Filipino and American soldiers were forced to march by the Japanese military. Bataan is home to the Dambana ng Kagitingan (Shrine of Valor), a national shrine that commemorates the bravery of soldiers who fought during the war. The province is also known for its ecological diversity, featuring natural attractions like the Mariveles Mountains and beautiful beaches along the coastline. Additionally, Bataan hosts the annual Bataan World War II Memorial commemorating the sacrifices made during the war, attracting both local and international visitors.
