Basic Information

Manila to Pangasinan is approximately 250 kilometers north of the capital, making it a popular destination for both short trips and longer vacations. The travel time usually ranges from 4 to 6 hours by car or bus, primarily via the Tarlac-Pangasinan-La Union Expressway (TPLEX). Pangasinan is famous for its stunning beaches, particularly in areas like Alaminos, known for the Hundred Islands National Park. The province is also renowned for its delicious seafood, local delicacies, and vibrant festivals such as the Pista'y Dayat (Sea Festival).

Trivia

The Manila to Pangasinan route has historical significance, as it was used during the Spanish colonial period for trade and transportation. Pangasinan is home to the Hundred Islands National Park, which consists of over 100 islands and islets, offering diverse ecosystems and recreational activities. The province is famous for its long-standing tradition of bangus (milkfish) farming, particularly in Dagupan, which is considered the "Bangus Capital of the World."
