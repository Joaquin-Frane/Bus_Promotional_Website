Basic Information

Manila to Legazpi is approximately a 10 to 12-hour drive, covering around 500 kilometers via the South Luzon Expressway (SLEX) and the Pan-Philippine Highway. Legazpi, the capital of Albay province, is renowned for its stunning views of Mayon Volcano, which is famous for its perfect cone shape and is an active stratovolcano. The city offers a rich cultural experience, with historical sites such as the Cagsawa Ruins, a relic of a church destroyed during a volcanic eruption in the 19th century. Additionally, Legazpi serves as a gateway to various natural attractions, including the picturesque beaches of Donsol and the beautiful landscapes of the surrounding region.

Trivia

The drive from Manila to Legazpi takes you through scenic landscapes, including lush mountains and coastal views, showcasing the beauty of the Bicol region. Legazpi is known for its proximity to Mayon Volcano, which is often considered the most photographed volcano in the Philippines due to its perfect cone shape and stunning backdrop. The city is also famous for the annual Ibalong Festival, celebrating the legendary Bicolano hero, Handiong, with colorful parades and cultural performances. Additionally, Legazpi has a rich history, with landmarks like the Cagsawa Ruins serving as a reminder of the devastating eruptions of Mayon Volcano in the past.
